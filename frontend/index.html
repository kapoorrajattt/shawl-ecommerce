Heritage & Craft

Our Tradition

🏔️

Mountain Roots

Our weavers have lived in the Kullu valley for generations. The patterns they weave are passed from grandparent to grandchild — a living language of wool and colour.

🐑

Pure Local Wool

We source wool exclusively from sheep reared in the high-altitude pastures of Himachal Pradesh. Natural fibres, natural warmth — no shortcuts.

🎨

Natural Dyes

Walnut shells, pomegranate rinds, indigo plants — our dye recipes are ancient. The colours you see in every shawl come straight from the Himalayan earth.

The Process

The Art of Weaving

1️⃣

SHEARING

Wool is hand-sheared from local sheep in spring, cleaned in mountain streams, and sun-dried.

2️⃣

SPINNING

Women spin wool into fine yarn on traditional charkhas. The thickness determines the final texture of the shawl.

3️⃣

DYEING

Yarn is dyed in natural pigments extracted from local plants and minerals, then dried in open mountain air.

4️⃣

WEAVING

Artisans weave on pit looms, interlocking hundreds of threads to create the iconic Kullu geometric patterns.

Who We Are

About Kullu Valley Handloom

Founded in the heart of the Kullu valley, we are a collective of over 200 artisan weavers spread across Kullu, Kinnaur, and Lahaul-Spiti.

Our mission is simple: bring authentic Himachali handloom directly to your doorstep — no middlemen, fair prices, and a living income for every weaver in our network.

Every purchase you make directly supports a mountain family and keeps a 400-year-old craft alive.
